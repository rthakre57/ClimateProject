Challenges and Limitations

Computational Complexity

  • Extensive computational resources, including high-performance computing clusters.
  • Efficient algorithms and numerical methods to reduce runtime.
  • Balancing model detail with computational feasibility to enable practical simulations.

Uncertainties in Model Inputs

  • Greenhouse gas emission trajectories.
  • Natural climate variability and external forcings.
  • Parameter values that are difficult to measure or estimate.
  • Limitations in observational data quality and coverage.

Dealing with Nonlinearity and Chaos

  • Sensitivity to initial conditions causing divergent model outcomes.
  • Complex feedback mechanisms that can amplify or dampen changes.
  • Challenges in long-term prediction accuracy and stability.

Interdisciplinary Barriers

  • Atmospheric science, oceanography, and ecology.
  • Mathematics, physics, and computer science.
  • Economics, social sciences, and policy studies.
  • Overcoming differences in terminology, methodologies, and data standards.
