Heat and Mass Transfer Equations

Heat and mass transfer processes are fundamental to the dynamics of the Earth's climate system. These processes govern the exchange of energy and matter between the atmosphere, oceans, cryosphere, and biosphere.

1. Heat Transfer Equation

The general form of the heat conduction (diffusion) equation in one dimension is:

$$\frac{\partial T(x,t)}{\partial t} = \kappa \frac{\partial^2 T(x,t)}{\partial x^2}$$

Where:

  • \( T(x,t) \) is temperature at position \( x \) and time \( t \),
  • \( \kappa \) is the thermal diffusivity,
  • \( \frac{\partial T}{\partial t} \) represents the temporal change of temperature,
  • \( \frac{\partial^2 T}{\partial x^2} \) is the spatial curvature of the temperature field.
Example: Surface Temperature Penetration into Soil

Assume a sinusoidal surface temperature variation:

$$T(0,t) = T_0 + A \cos(\omega t)$$

Then the solution of the heat equation is:

$$T(x,t) = T_0 + A e^{-\sqrt{\frac{\omega}{2\kappa}}x} \cos\left(\omega t - \sqrt{\frac{\omega}{2\kappa}}x \right)$$

This shows how temperature oscillations decrease and lag with depth.

2. Mass Transfer Equation

Mass transport in the atmosphere or ocean is often modeled using the advection-diffusion equation:

$$\frac{\partial C}{\partial t} + \vec{u} \cdot \nabla C = D \nabla^2 C + S$$

Where:

  • \( C \) is the concentration of a substance (e.g., water vapor, CO\(_2\)),
  • \( \vec{u} \) is the velocity field (e.g., wind or ocean current),
  • \( D \) is the diffusivity coefficient,
  • \( S \) represents sources or sinks (e.g., emissions or absorption).
Example: CO2 Transport in the Atmosphere

Assuming steady-state, 1D transport with a source term \( S(x) \):

$$u \frac{dC}{dx} = D \frac{d^2C}{dx^2} + S(x)$$

This equation can be solved numerically or analytically under specific boundary conditions to understand how carbon dioxide disperses.

3. Coupling with Climate Models

Heat and mass transfer equations are numerically integrated as part of larger climate models. Their interaction determines:

  • Temperature gradients in soil and ocean layers.
  • Humidity and precipitation patterns.
  • Greenhouse gas concentrations and transport.
