Coupled Subsystems and Integrated Models

Climate systems are inherently complex and consist of various interconnected subsystems including the atmosphere, oceans, land surface, cryosphere, and biosphere. Coupled climate models aim to simulate interactions among these components using a unified framework, often resulting in Integrated Assessment Models (IAMs).

1. Mathematical Framework

Each subsystem is governed by a set of differential equations, and coupling is achieved through interface variables that exchange fluxes of energy, mass, or momentum. Let us define:

  • \(\mathbf{A}(t)\): State vector for atmosphere
  • \(\mathbf{O}(t)\): State vector for ocean
  • \(\mathbf{L}(t)\): Land surface variables
  • \(\mathbf{B}(t)\): Biosphere (e.g., carbon cycle)
  • \(\mathbf{F}_{ij}\): Flux from subsystem \(i\) to \(j\)
\[ \frac{d\mathbf{A}}{dt} = \mathcal{F}_A(\mathbf{A}, \mathbf{O}, \mathbf{L}, \mathbf{B}, \mathbf{F}_{OA}, \mathbf{F}_{LA}) \] \[ \frac{d\mathbf{O}}{dt} = \mathcal{F}_O(\mathbf{O}, \mathbf{A}, \mathbf{F}_{AO}) \] \[ \frac{d\mathbf{L}}{dt} = \mathcal{F}_L(\mathbf{L}, \mathbf{A}, \mathbf{F}_{AL}) \] \[ \frac{d\mathbf{B}}{dt} = \mathcal{F}_B(\mathbf{B}, \mathbf{A}, \mathbf{L}, \mathbf{F}_{LB}, \mathbf{F}_{AB}) \]

2. Example: Carbon-Climate Feedback System

Consider a simplified coupled model of the climate and carbon cycle:

\[ \frac{dT}{dt} = \alpha F_{CO_2} - \beta T \] \[ \frac{dC}{dt} = E(t) - \gamma T - \delta C \]
  • \(T(t)\): global mean temperature
  • \(C(t)\): atmospheric CO\(_2\) concentration
  • \(F_{CO_2} = \lambda \log\left(\frac{C}{C_0}\right)\): radiative forcing
  • \(E(t)\): anthropogenic CO\(_2\) emissions
  • \(\alpha, \beta, \gamma, \delta, \lambda\): empirical parameters

4. Applications

  • Earth System Models (ESMs)
  • Integrated Assessment Models (IAMs) for climate policy
  • Long-term projections of climate and socio-economic dynamics
