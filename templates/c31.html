Carbon Cycle Modeling

The carbon cycle describes the movement of carbon among Earthâ€™s major reservoirs: the atmosphere, ocean, terrestrial biosphere, and lithosphere. Mathematical models help simulate carbon fluxes and predict future CO2 levels under different scenarios.

1. Box Model Formulation

One common approach is the compartment (box) model, where each reservoir is represented as a "box" with carbon fluxes between them.

Let:

  • \( C_A(t) \) = atmospheric CO\(_2\) concentration (in GtC)
  • \( C_L(t) \) = land biosphere carbon
  • \( C_O(t) \) = ocean carbon
  • \( F_{LA}, F_{AL} \) = fluxes between land and atmosphere
  • \( F_{AO}, F_{OA} \) = fluxes between atmosphere and ocean
  • \( E(t) \) = anthropogenic CO\(_2\) emissions
\[ \frac{dC_A}{dt} = E(t) + F_{LA}(C_L) + F_{OA}(C_O) - F_{AL}(C_A) - F_{AO}(C_A) \] \[ \frac{dC_L}{dt} = F_{AL}(C_A) - F_{LA}(C_L) \] \[ \frac{dC_O}{dt} = F_{AO}(C_A) - F_{OA}(C_O) \]

2. Example: Simple Linear Flux Model

Assume linear fluxes and a constant emission rate \( E(t) = E_0 \):

\[ F_{AL} = k_{AL} C_A, \quad F_{LA} = k_{LA} C_L \] \[ F_{AO} = k_{AO} C_A, \quad F_{OA} = k_{OA} C_O \]

Substituting into the ODEs:

\[ \frac{dC_A}{dt} = E_0 + k_{LA} C_L + k_{OA} C_O - (k_{AL} + k_{AO}) C_A \] \[ \frac{dC_L}{dt} = k_{AL} C_A - k_{LA} C_L \] \[ \frac{dC_O}{dt} = k_{AO} C_A - k_{OA} C_O \]

These coupled ODEs can be solved numerically using methods like Runge-Kutta to analyze carbon concentration trends over time.

3. Application in Climate Models

Carbon cycle models are crucial for:

  • Estimating atmospheric CO\(_2\) in emission scenarios (e.g., RCP/SSP)
  • Assessing carbon sinks and sources
  • Coupling with climate models to account for feedbacks
